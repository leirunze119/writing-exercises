In this paper, we give new differentially private algorithms for counting distinct elements. While there has been prior work on this problem, their space-utility trade-offs are exponentially worse than that of non-private variants. We bridge this gap by achieving nearly optimal space-utility trade-off, assuming the existence of an ideal uniform random hash function. This assumption can be removed at some additional space cost.
