图8显示了运行端到端heavy hitters协议的每个客户端每台服务器的总通信成本。在此实验中，客户端从参数为1.03的Zipf分布中采样字符串。这种“幂律”分布很自然地出现在网络设置中，我们保守地选择参数（即自然分布比我们期望的更接近均匀分布），这可能会低估我们系统的性能。在此实验中，服务器搜索0.1％以上的客户端持有的字符串。在我们的方案中，每个客户端的总通信量为数十KB。图8还估计了使用基准方案和我们的方案来计算heavy hitters的美元成本随客户数量的变化。我们的方案便宜了大约两个数量级。

Figure 8 shows the total communication cost per server per client of running our end-to-end heavy hitters protocol. In this experiment, clients sample their strings from a Zipf distribution with parameter 1.03. This type of “power-law” distribution arises naturally in network settings and we choose the parameter conservatively (i.e., the distribution is closer to uniform than we would expect in nature), which likely gives an underestimate of our system’s performance. In this experiment, servers search for strings that more than 0.1% of clients hold. In our schemes, the total communication per client is tens of kilobytes. Figure 8 also estimates the dollar cost of computing private heavy hitters using the baseline scheme and our scheme, as the number of clients varies. Our scheme is roughly two orders of magnitude less expensive.
